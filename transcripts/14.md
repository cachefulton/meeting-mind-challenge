## What "insights" could add

Right now the schema extracts **what happened** (summary, actions, decisions, questions). What's missing is **what it means** тАФ the analytical layer. An `insights` field could capture:

- **Key themes/topics** тАФ what the meeting was fundamentally about (e.g., "notification fatigue", "API performance")
- **Risks or concerns** raised but not resolved
- **Sentiment/mood** тАФ was this a productive meeting, a tense one, a brainstorming session?
- **Participant dynamics** тАФ who drove the conversation, who was assigned the most, who raised concerns
- **Follow-up recommendations** тАФ what the team should probably do next beyond what was explicitly stated

This moves the tool from a **transcription reformatter** into an **analysis engine** тАФ which is exactly the "beyond a simple API wrapper" signal the rubric calls for.

**2. Generic disambiguation rules in the prompt** тАФ not sample-specific, but covering the challenge classes:

- **Commitments vs suggestions**: Only extract as an action item if someone explicitly commits or is assigned. Phrases like "maybe we should" or "we could" are observations/open questions, not action items.
- **Decisions vs discussion**: A decision requires group convergence or explicit approval. One person's opinion is not a decision. Look for confirmation signals ("agreed", "sounds good", "let's do that").
- **Emotional context vs actionable content**: Frustration, praise, and venting should inform `sentiment` and `insights`, not pollute `actionItems` or `decisions`.
- **Implicit ownership**: Infer assignee only when the speaker volunteers ("I'll do that") or is directly asked and accepts. Don't guess.
- **Priority inference**: High = has a deadline or is blocking others. Medium = committed to but no urgency. Low = stretch goal or "if time allows."

**3. Two-pass analysis (optional but high signal)** тАФ The first call extracts structured data. A lightweight second call (or even the same call with a richer prompt) synthesizes the `insights` array тАФ themes, risks, and follow-up recommendations based on the extracted data. This is architecturally distinct from a wrapper because the second pass reasons *about the extraction*, not just the transcript.

Alternatively, a single-pass approach with the enhanced schema and prompt may be sufficient тАФ Gemini handles complex schemas well, and a single pass is simpler. The two-pass version is more defensible but costs latency.

**4. Pre-processing** тАФ Before sending to the LLM:
- **Detect transcript format** (timestamped vs plain text vs speaker-labeled)
- **Extract participant list** from the header/content as context for the prompt
- **Token estimation** тАФ if the transcript exceeds ~80% of context window, chunk with overlap and merge results

**5. Post-processing** тАФ After the LLM response:
- Deduplicate action items that say the same thing differently
- Validate assignee names against the extracted participant list
- Retry with a more specific prompt if key fields are empty (e.g., 0 action items on a long transcript is suspicious)
